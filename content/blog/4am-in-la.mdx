title: 4am in los angeles
date: 10/10/2025
description: on love and the meaning of life. i dont think im fully right but being right is not the goal. this is to capture a feeling
---

some may say that the path to happiness is to experience life more. try all the extreme sports, date all different kinds of people, travel to different places. by no means are these pursuits wrong, i in fact fully support them. but i'm dubious of the view that experiences equate to happiness, especially when it becomes an indulgence of needing "more". plato has this excellent analogy of desires as a thirst or an itch: is the man who constantly feels compelled to drink or scratch an itch, happy? that's a reason why we say addicts are not free, because they are slaves to their desires.

but that is not to say you should not go out and live. on the contrary, that's exactly what you should do: experience things just enough to understand what they really are. go skydiving once, go see a superstar once, go fuck a supermodel once, go to an exotic country once — well maybe a couple more times, traveling is fun. fall uncontrollably in love once. achieve great success once. to have just enough in each category to say: so that's what it feels like. then a few more times to realize: oh it's just that. a huge part of being human shares a repeated pattern, and when you recognize there's nothing that is "that special", you see how accumulating more moments is not an end goal. they are means to help you overcome each certain 执念*. if i never had a love story like the movies, i would still be craving it. i would think that's the missing piece for me to be complete. if i'd never been to the US, i would spend my life fantasizing how much better it would be than a life constrained in china. and i will never regret making those decisions and choosing this life, even though they didn't lead me to that utopia i imagined. the real value is to live through things to understand that there is nothing in the human experience that's going to serve as an ultimate answer.

today i want to talk about love. above was a very roundabout intro, but still relevant because love is (in my opinion still without a doubt) the greatest human experience of all. so if there is no ultimate answer to life, does love at least come close to an admirable response? i've heard a lot of people say that, and i emotionally resonate with this heavily. i've glimpsed eternity in my lover's eyes, for a split second in space and time. 

but the greatest tragedy of humans is when two people try to become one, yet recognize the futility in the process, as they will always remain two lone individuals. humans are no more different than animals, just so egotistical that they'd build a babel tower to reach god. we have an obsession with eternity, of heaven, of happily ever after; these big words are the wishful projection of this moment onto infinity. people often end up being slaves of love, trying to experience that high again, just because for that split second it felt so amazing, so ethereal, that we forget what pain and boredom feel like. the loverboys and lovergirls fit into this archetype and become movie protagonists. the notebook is amazing because it is 90 minutes; outside of the 90 minutes it's the repeated cycle of an unresolved addiction to be held. a spark of magic is never meant to last, unless you commit to the repetition just to catch that feeling again.

i currently hold a belief that love is just another 执念*, and the most powerful of it all. ultimately, we are not savable through another human being. of course my beliefs change, the heart is easily swayed, i might believe in this santa claus story of love again one day; but tonight, i think there is a reason why salvation can only be achieved through god. to pour your hopes of filling your shadow into the image of a savior, then try to fit another fellow human being into that mold, is a false exit. the only way to be freed from the shadow is to face it yourself.

isn't life very sisyphean? i feel like i always come back to this. staying in a relationship for stability, keep fixing the same problems, accept the natural erosion of two identities to trade for harmony, is sisyphean; exiting a relationship to focus on yourself, face the constant themes of shadows, come to terms with life's eternal solitude to remain independent, is sisyphean. which one to choose is the question, and an even greater question would be: do we even get to choose, or are we mere bearers of what has already been laid before us. welcome to another day of rolling stones, i should go to bed, and the sun will rise again tomorrow.

---

执念: i left this here because this chinese word captures something specific that english doesn't quite have a single word for. understand it as an obsessive attachment or fixation one can't let go of, often something you know might not be good for you, but emotionally still bound to it. buddhist philosophy talks about letting go of 执念 to achieve inner peace
